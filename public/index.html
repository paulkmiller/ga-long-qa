How much money goes to transit in Georgia?

Last year, total transit investment from all three levels of government came out to almost $240 million. In aggregate, Georgia had the third-highest level of transit investment in the Southeast, after Florida and North Carolina. However, 78% of those funds came from the federal government, the 4th highest percentage in the nation.

Federal Transit Investment in the Southeast Region

On a per-person basis, Georgia receives more federal dollars than any other state in the region. More than double what Alabama, Mississippi, and South Carolina receive.

State Transit Investment in the Southeast

Georgia invested $1.15 per person, far below the regional average of $4.85 per person. Nationally, states spend an average of $35.80 per person.

Comparing Federal and State Investment

Georgia invests 6 cents for every transit $1 invested by the federal government, the second lowest in the region, beating out Alabama at 4/10 of a penny. Nationally, states put up 99 cents for every federal transit $1.

Local Transit Investment in the Southeast

Georgia's local governments invest $4.14 per person, ranking 6th in the region, and a far cry from the national average of $35.80. Florida is the only state in the neighborhood.

Comparing Local and State Investment

When compared to state funding, Georgia's local governments invest at a higher rate than most of the region, with Alabama leading the pack simply because the state only invested $143,000. Ouch.

The Big Picture: How are transit expenses paid for nationally?

Nationally, the federal, state, and local government take on a virtually equal share of nationwide transit spending, with transit passenger fare revenue contributing an equal portion to the total.

Who bears the brunt of transit expenses in Georgia?

In Georgia, lack of state and local funding support has left transit passengers and the federal government holding the bag. Driving up fares and making it even more difficult for residents to use transit.
